# Advantages & Disadvantages — Type-Specific Evaluation Guide

## Prompt identification

Signals: "What are the advantages and disadvantages?", "Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?", "Is this a positive or negative development?"

## Task Response priorities

| Priority | What to assess |
|----------|----------------|
| **High** | Both advantages AND disadvantages addressed (unless prompt asks only one side) |
| **Medium** | Clear verdict when asked "outweigh" — must compare and decide |
| **Low** | Only advantages listed; no disadvantages (or vice versa) |

## Required structure

### "Outweigh" prompts
1. Introduction — Paraphrase + thesis on which side outweighs
2. Body 1 — Advantages (developed)
3. Body 2 — Disadvantages (developed)
4. Conclusion — Confirm which outweighs and why

### "Discuss both" without outweigh
- May use one body for advantages, one for disadvantages
- Conclusion may summarise balance without forced verdict

## Common TR weaknesses

- Listing bullet-style points without development
- No answer to "outweigh" question
- One-sided essay
- Conclusion introduces new advantage/disadvantage not discussed in body

## Lexical/cohesion focus

Advantage: benefit, positive aspect, favourable, enables, contributes to
Disadvantage: drawback, downside, detrimental, poses a risk, leads to
Comparison: outweigh, on balance, nevertheless, despite these benefits

## Model essay tone

Analytical, evaluative when "outweigh" required. Target **280+ words**.
