# Opinion Essay — Type-Specific Evaluation Guide

## Prompt identification

Signals: "To what extent do you agree or disagree?", "Do you agree or disagree?", "What is your opinion?"

## Task Response priorities

| Priority | What to assess |
|----------|----------------|
| **High** | Clear, consistent position throughout; direct answer to extent question |
| **Medium** | Developed arguments with examples/reasoning supporting the stance |
| **Low** | Discussing both sides equally without clear personal position (unless partial agreement structure) |

## Required structure

1. **Introduction** — Paraphrase + **clear thesis** (agree/disagree/partially agree)
2. **Body 1** — Strongest reason supporting position
3. **Body 2** — Second reason OR concession + rebuttal (for partial agreement)
4. **Conclusion** — Restate position; no new arguments

## Position types

- **Full agree/disagree**: Both body paragraphs support same side
- **Partial agreement**: One body for one view, one for counter, conclusion clarifies balance

## Common TR weaknesses

- No clear opinion in introduction
- Body contradicts thesis
- Essay becomes discussion of both views without stating own view
- Ignoring "to what extent" — must address degree of agreement

## Lexical/cohesion focus

Opinion markers: I believe, In my view, It is argued that, Clearly, Undoubtedly
Avoid: "Some people think… Some people think…" without synthesis

## Model essay tone

Assertive, consistent stance, formal academic. Target **280+ words**.
