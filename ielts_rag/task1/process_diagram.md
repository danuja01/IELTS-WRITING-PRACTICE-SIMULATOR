# Process Diagram — Type-Specific Evaluation Guide

## Visual identification

Diagram showing **stages of a process** (natural cycle, manufacturing, recycling). Linear or cyclical flow with arrows.

## Task Achievement priorities

| Priority | What to assess |
|----------|----------------|
| **High** | All major stages covered; correct sequence; clear start and end |
| **Medium** | Logical grouping of related stages |
| **Low** | Speculative causes/reasons not shown in diagram |

## Overview expectations

Summarise the **overall process** (e.g. "Overall, the process consists of seven stages, beginning with harvesting and ending with distribution."). No stage-by-stage detail in overview.

## Body paragraph grouping

- **Early stages** vs **later stages**
- **Preparation** vs **processing** vs **output**
- For cycles: **first half of cycle** vs **second half**

## Tense and voice guidance

**Present simple passive** is standard:

"is harvested", "is transported", "is heated", "is filtered", "undergoes", "results in"

Do not mix past and present without reason. Do not explain **why** stages occur unless shown.

## Lexical resource — sequencing

first, initially, subsequently, following this, once … has been …, the next stage, finally, at this point, before being, after being

## Common weaknesses

- Missing or reordering stages
- Active voice when passive expected
- Explaining causes not in diagram
- No overview of total stages

## Data accuracy checks

Stage order and labels must match diagram. Skipped steps = **Task achievement** issue.

## Model report tone

Procedural, sequential, passive. Target **160–190 words**.
