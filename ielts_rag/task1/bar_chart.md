# Bar Chart — Type-Specific Evaluation Guide

## Visual identification

Vertical or horizontal bars comparing **categories**. May be **static** (single time point) or **dynamic** (multiple time periods grouped).

## Task Achievement priorities

| Priority | What to assess |
|----------|----------------|
| **High** | Rankings (highest/lowest); significant gaps between categories; logical grouping |
| **Medium** | Similar-sized categories compared together |
| **Low** | Every bar described in isolation |

## Dynamic vs static bar charts

- **With time periods**: trend language allowed ("X rose from… to… between 2010 and 2020")
- **Single time point**: use **comparison language only** — NOT rise/fall unless comparing two time bars

## Overview expectations

State the **dominant pattern**: which category leads, largest gap, or general ranking. Static charts: "Country A had the highest consumption while Country D recorded the lowest."

## Body paragraph grouping

- Group **similar categories** (e.g. top performers vs lower performers)
- Compare **production vs consumption** pairs when relevant
- Avoid bar-by-bar sequential listing

## Lexical resource — comparison vocabulary

accounted for, stood at, represented, exceeded, surpassed, recorded, was considerably higher than, was comparable to, ranked first, the second largest, approximately twice as much as

## Common weaknesses

- Treating static bar chart as line graph (inappropriate trend verbs)
- Describing bars one by one without grouping
- Missing highest/lowest or key contrasts
- Repeating identical sentence patterns

## Data accuracy checks

Bar heights must match stated figures. Confusing categories or units = **Data accuracy** mistakes.

## Model report tone

Comparative, ranking-focused. Target **160–190 words**.
