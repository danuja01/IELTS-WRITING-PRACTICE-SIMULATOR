# Pie Chart — Type-Specific Evaluation Guide

## Visual identification

Circular chart showing **proportions/percentages** of a whole at typically **one point in time**.

## Task Achievement priorities

| Priority | What to assess |
|----------|----------------|
| **High** | Largest/smallest segments; dominant share; relative proportions |
| **Medium** | Meaningful segment-to-segment comparisons |
| **Low** | Listing every percentage without interpretation |

## Overview expectations

Identify **dominant and minor shares** (e.g. "Food accounted for the largest proportion while Transport represented the smallest share."). No need for every percentage in overview.

## Body paragraph grouping

- Dominant segments vs minor segments
- Related categories grouped (e.g. leisure activities together)
- Compare **pairs of similar-sized** slices

## Language focus

Use **proportion language**, not trend language:

proportion, percentage, share, accounted for, constituted, made up, represented, the majority of, a minority of, just over a quarter, slightly less than half

## Common weaknesses

- Using trend verbs (rose/fell) — pie charts are usually static
- Treating like bar chart without proportion framing
- Missing the largest contributor
- Mechanical percentage listing

## Data accuracy checks

Percentages should sum to ~100%. Wrong segment values = **Data accuracy** mistakes.

## Model report tone

Proportion-focused, selective. Target **160–190 words**.
