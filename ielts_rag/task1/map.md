# Map — Type-Specific Evaluation Guide

## Visual identification

Maps show **spatial layout** and/or **changes over time** (before/after, two dates). Focus is on **location and transformation**, not numerical trends.

## Task Achievement priorities

| Priority | What to assess |
|----------|----------------|
| **High** | Major developments (construction, demolition, expansion); overview of transformation; unchanged features mentioned |
| **Medium** | Grouping changes by area (north/residential/commercial) |
| **Low** | Listing every small structure without synthesis |

## Overview expectations

Describe the **overall transformation** (e.g. "The village developed from a rural settlement into a residential area with expanded infrastructure."). No compass directions required in overview unless central to change.

## Body paragraph grouping

- By **geographic area** (north vs south, centre vs outskirts)
- By **type of change** (new housing vs new transport)
- **Before/after**: compare same zones across time periods

## Tense and voice guidance

| Context | Language |
|---------|----------|
| Past map date | Past simple passive: "was built", "was demolished" |
| Present-day map | Present perfect: "has been converted into" |
| Location | "to the north of", "adjacent to", "in the centre of" — use compass if shown |

## Lexical resource — change vocabulary

underwent development, was constructed, was expanded, was relocated, remained unchanged, was converted into, was replaced by, was demolished, was established, a new … was added, the area was redeveloped

**Do NOT** use graph trend verbs (rose, fluctuated) for maps.

## Common weaknesses

- Using data-chart vocabulary on maps
- Missing unchanged features
- No overview of overall development
- Opinion ("the park is beautiful")
- Ignoring compass/orientation when provided

## Data accuracy checks

Verify what was added, removed, or moved matches the map. Invented landmarks = **Data accuracy** mistakes.

## Model report tone

Descriptive, spatial, passive-voice heavy. Target **170–200 words**.
